II
The Ranch

A place for family,
held open for the
right kind of quiet.

The Lavender family keeps a working parcel in Linn County, Kansas — hayfields, ponds, a small herd, a stand of hardwood at the north line. The ranch was built slowly, by hand, and is opened by invitation.

Ninety minutes south of Kansas City, the land begins to roll.

The Osage Cuestas. Hayfields, ponds, a stand of hardwood at the north line. In October, Sugar Mound burns red. In March, the fields are the color of pale butter.

Not a venue. Not, most days, a business. A piece of ground a family looks after — and that, on occasion, looks after other people.

Gatherings

What the ranch
does, when it does
anything at all.

The ranch is a private place. From time to time it opens, by invitation, for reasons the family considers worth opening it for.

V · i

Equine stays — by referral.

A quiet arrangement for travelling sport horse operations moving through the region. Stall space, turnout, ground, and a clean place for the people who bring them. Peer-to-peer; not published.
